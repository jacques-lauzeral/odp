= 4D Trajectory
:notitle:

The 4D Trajectory (4DT) constitutes a cornerstone of future Network operations and is a key enabler for enhanced
predictability, efficiency, and collaborative decision‑making across the ATM Network. Within this context, the NM 4DT
domain group is responsible for addressing all topics related to trajectory management, with a particular focus on
trajectory‑related operational aspects embedded in the wider iCDM framework.

The 4DT group provides the primary forum for discussing Operational Needs (ONs) and Operational Requirements (ORs)
within NM, to be shared via the ODIP with stakeholders under the relevant working arrangements. Close coordination with
other domain groups, such as Flow, Rerouting, and Airspace (iDL), is key to ensuring transparency and cross‑domain
understanding.

The work of the 4DT group is focused on the implementation of the Network 4D Trajectory Concept of Operations, from
which the relevant ONs have been identified. This foundation is complemented by any other emerging concepts, ensuring
consistency and alignment with ongoing developments at Network level. Once implemented and operational in the NM system,
the Network 4D Trajectory Concept of Operations will give NM the key role to serve as 4D trajectory planner and
coordinator, responsible for the validation, negotiation, and timely sharing of consolidated flight data with
stakeholders, complementary to ATC processes.

The operational scope covered under the 4DT domain includes the development and evolution of advanced inputs to enrich
the NM trajectory, from pre-agreed transfer conditions between ATS units in their Letters of Agreement to further
tactical updates from ATC units as flights transit over Europe. The scope also covers the output of this enriched
trajectory with a 4D Network trajectory service that will offer this high-quality trajectory to all interested
operational parties, that will become the European benchmark, supporting local flow management and strategic ATC.

Several other enabling needs to improve the NM trajectory are also included in the work of the group, such as the
management of preferred trajectories throughout the flight lifecycle, VFR and OAT operations, high-altitude and new
entrants.

The 4DT scope outlined above translates into a set of 34 Operational Needs and over 100 Operational Requirements. To
help readers find their way through them, the chapter is organised around eight operational themes - broadly following
the path of a flight through the 4DT lifecycle, complemented by transversal topics.

A first group covers xref:4dt/flight_planning_services_ff_ice/index.adoc[flight planning services under the FF-ICE framework]: how Flight Plans are filed, validated, updated
and cancelled, and how restrictions, constraints and ATFM measures are returned to the operator.

A second group addresses xref:4dt/trajectory_inputs_from_operational_stakeholders/index.adoc[trajectory inputs from operational stakeholders] - the information that feeds the Network 4D
trajectory ahead of and during execution: pre-agreed transfer conditions in Letters of Agreement, AU-preferred
trajectories, and aircraft performance data.

A third group covers the xref:4dt/construction_of_the_network_4d_trajectory/index.adoc[construction of the Network 4D Trajectory] itself: how NM builds, maintains and distributes one
consolidated 4D trajectory per flight, kept consistent throughout the flight lifecycle.

A fourth group describes the xref:4dt/cdm_strategic_revision_platform/index.adoc[CDM strategic revision platform] - the collaborative mechanism for AUs, ANSPs and NM to
negotiate strategic trajectory revisions before tactical execution: proposal generation, counterproposal handling,
Network evaluation, and agreement distribution.

A fifth group covers xref:4dt/tactical_updates_from_airborne_and_ground_sources/index.adoc[tactical updates from airborne and ground sources] - real-time enrichment of the trajectory from two
complementary streams: EPP data via the ADS-C Common Service, and trajectory updates from ATC via OLDI exchanges over
SWIM.

A sixth group addresses xref:4dt/impact_assessment_and_rerouting_support/index.adoc[impact assessment and rerouting support] - forward-looking support to AUs and ANSPs: Network
impact assessment for flight-level and route choices, smart rerouting replays, and the integration of MET data into
rerouting proposals.

A seventh group covers xref:4dt/vfr_flight_plan_handling/index.adoc[VFR Flight Plan handling] - the full chain from the registration of responsible AROs and trusted
submitters, through ARO access to the NM system, to NM-side validation, handling and distribution.

A final group brings together xref:4dt/other_flight_categories/index.adoc[other flight categories] that require dedicated handling within the 4DT framework: mixed
iOAT/GAT operations, military and non-military iOAT, High-Altitude Operations, and IFR RPAS.

_Within each section, Operational Needs are presented first, followed by the Operational Requirements that implement
them._
