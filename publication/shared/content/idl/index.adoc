= Airspace (iDL)
:notitle:

== Introduction

The iDL is the ATM/AIM data component of the integrated Network Management (iNM) Programme — the digital transformation by which NM and its stakeholders will work together from 2029.
It collects, consolidates and provides the aeronautical, airspace, airport, meteorological and other data required for Network operations across all flight phases.
It replaces the current fragmentation between the European AIS Database (EAD) and the Central Airspace and Capacity Database (CACD), which today maintain overlapping but inconsistent data through largely independent processes.
The iDL ensures a single, consistent representation of each operational reality, maintained collaboratively through cooperative decision-making with the Network stakeholders responsible for that data.

The Airspace (iDL) chapter is the foundation of all other operational chapters in the ODIP.
The data it describes — airports, airspaces, routes, sectors, navaids, restrictions, traffic flows, and the rules and processes that govern them — underpins Flight, Flow, ASM/ATFCM Integration, Airport, Crisis, and FAAS.
Every flight planning decision, every ATFCM measure, every airspace allocation, every coordination act between centres ultimately rests on the quality, currency, and consistency of this data.

The chapter is anchored in the link:https://www.eurocontrol.int/publication/network-idl-conops[Network iDL CONOPS] (Edition 2.1, 16/02/2024), which establishes the integrated Data Layer as a foundational component of iNM.
Strategic alignment derives from the Network Strategy Plan, the European ATM Master Plan, the Network Functions Implementing Regulation (EU 2019/123), and Commission Implementing Regulation (EU) 2017/373 on ATM/AIM data provision.

The iDL is conceptually denser than the operational domains downstream of it: it spans rules, model, process and domain-specific applications.
The chapter is therefore organised as one anchor introduction followed by six sub-domain chapters, each a self-contained document focused on one distinct concern.

== A layered architecture: ADMM, ADM, AD

Three layers organise the iDL world.

The *integrated Data Layer ATM/AIM Data (iDL-AD)* is the actual operational data: airports, airspaces, routes, navaids, sectors, traffic flows and related entities that NM systems and stakeholders work with day to day.

The *iDL-AD Data Model (iDL-ADM)* is the formal model that defines the iDL-AD: it specifies the types of data that may exist, the attributes and relations of each type, and the validation rules that ensure correctness.

The *iDL-AD Data Metamodel (iDL-ADMM)* is the model of the model: it specifies how the iDL-ADM itself must be structured, what kinds of elements it may contain, and how those elements may evolve over time.

These three layers nest cleanly: the iDL-ADMM governs the iDL-ADM; the iDL-ADM defines the iDL-AD.

== What the iDL-ADM is made of

The iDL-ADM is constructed from four kinds of elements, each playing a distinct role.
*Basic Types* name atomic values — codes, measures, date or position pairs — together with their format and validation constraints.
*Structure Types* name structured fragments — an airspace volume, a route segment's availability, a runway threshold — that have no independent identity and exist as components of entities.
*Entity Types* name the operational concepts — Airports, Routes, Airspaces, Navaids — and carry identity, attributes, relations and lifecycle.
*Network Validation Rules* express constraints spanning more than one entity.

== Dynamicity and updatability

Two related but distinct concepts govern how iDL-AD evolves over time.
*Dynamicity* is an operational validation rule constraining how frequently a value may legitimately change (frozen, static at AIRAC switch, dynamic ad-hoc, dynamic on schedule).
*Updatability* is a transaction rule constraining when a data transaction may be submitted to the operational iDL-AD (non-updatable, AIRAC-only, data-amendment).
The iDL-ADM treats them as separate orthogonal properties.

_Figure 9.3 — Dynamicity (operational validation rule: how frequently a value may change) and Updatability (transaction rule: when a transaction may be submitted) are distinct, orthogonal concepts._

== Regulated and Effective Geometry

Many iDL-AD entity types have geographic geometry.
The iDL-ADM separates two views.
*Regulated Geometry* is defined by the relevant authority and may use non-computable constructs suitable for AIS publication.
*Effective Geometry* is optimised for automated processing by Network flight and flow systems and may be auto-derived from regulated geometry — though the data provider retains override authority.
A compatibility check ensures the two remain operationally consistent.

== Editable and published forms

The iDL-ADM is maintained in two coordinated forms.
The *iDL-ADM/E* (Editable form) is used during model elaboration and references external sources such as the AIXM Conceptual Model rather than duplicating their content.
The *iDL-ADM/P* (Published form), derived from the editable form by resolution and merge, is self-contained and is the authoritative form exposed to stakeholders.
Web documentation, filtered views, and exchange models are all derived from the published form.

== A digital, collaborative process: the iDL-ADP

Alongside the layered model, the iDL introduces a single, unified *iDL-AD Process (iDL-ADP)*: the digital, collaborative process through which iDL data is elaborated, integrated, published and released to operations.
The iDL-ADP replaces the current fragmented processes with a single process organised around the *iDL Project* — within which all data changes are scoped, coordinated, assessed and released — supported by explicit roles, in-process communication mechanisms, and a transparent lifecycle.

== Sequencing: ADP first to deploy

The iDL is delivered stepwise over several years.
The first iDL evolution that operational stakeholders will experience is the *iDL-ADP*: the new process for elaborating data is introduced before the underlying iDL-ADM transitions are complete.
Subsequent iDL-ADM transitions then proceed slice by slice, each a planned operational change with its own scope, target wave and stakeholder consultation.

== Sub-domain chapters

The Airspace (iDL) chapter is delivered as seven sub-domain chapters, presented in didactic order — rules, then model, then process, then interfaces, then specific applications.

*§9.1 — Data Metamodel (ADMM)*: the rules governing how the iDL data model itself must be structured and how it may evolve.

*§9.2 — Data Model (ADM)*: ADM v1.0 consolidates content from multiple existing source models — including the AIXM Conceptual Model, AIXM extensions, AIXM–EAD business rules, and the current NM CACD and RAD models — into a single, unified, authoritative reference.

*§9.3 — Data Process (ADP)*: the digital, collaborative process for elaborating, integrating, publishing and releasing iDL data. The ADP is the first iDL evolution stakeholders will experience operationally.

*§9.4 — iDL Interfaces*: the surfaces through which stakeholders reach iDL-AD content — the NMUI for human users and the NM B2B interface for system-to-system integration.

*§9.5 — Airspace Utilisation Rules and Availability (AURA)*: the airspace utilisation rules and route availability that govern how Network airspace may be used by traffic, including the Route Availability Document (RAD) and associated additional rules.

*§9.6 — Letters of Agreement (LoA)*: coordination agreements between adjacent control centres covering exchange, transfer and procedural arrangements relevant to Network operations.

*§9.7 — Transponder Code Function (TCF)*: scarce-resource management for SSR transponder codes, including the Code Allocation List, Mode S eligibility, and the Centralised Code Assignment and Management System (CCAMS).

A Weather sub-domain may follow in a future edition.

== Reference documents

*Network iDL CONOPS* (Edition 2.1, 16/02/2024) — the authoritative concept of operations for the iDL, defining its objectives, scope, stakeholders, and the operational concepts that organise the sub-domains.

*AIXM Conceptual Model* — the ICAO standard Aeronautical Information Exchange Model (AIXM 5.1.1) and its extensions, used as the basis for the iDL-ADM where applicable.

*ERNIP* (European Route Network Improvement Plan), Parts 1 to 4 — for airspace design methodology, route definitions, airspace management procedures, and the Route Availability Document.
