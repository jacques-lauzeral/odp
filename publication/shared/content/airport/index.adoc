= Airport
:notitle:

This chapter sets out the Operational Needs and Operational Requirements relating to the Airport domain — covering
airport integration into the Network, airport information and monitoring, diversion handling, environmental performance,
and the supporting Tower user interface.

The chapter content is grounded in the iNM Flow CONOPS, iDL CONOPS, 4DT CONOPS and ASM/ATFCM CONOPS, which collectively
cover airport-related operational evolutions; the airport area does not maintain its own CONOPS document. The Airport
Drafting Group actively interacts with the Flow, iDL, 4DT, ASM/ATFCM and Performance Drafting Groups to coordinate
interfaces and resolve cross-domain dependencies.

Extending the integration of Airport Operations Plans with the Network Operations Plan, in line with
Common Project One (AF4), to improve Network resilience, operational efficiency and traffic predictability through
stronger AOP-NOP data exchange — including curfew information and alerting, ELDT source provision, and ATFCM regulation
impact prediction.

Integrating airports of all sizes into the Network through A-CDM, AAT and RNI concepts and through direct data exchange,
with the supporting operational services.

Displaying and reporting airport information that may have an impact on airport or Network operations.

Supporting airport operators' cooperation with NM through the Airport Function position, with access to demand-capacity
imbalances, regulations, weather, airport events and slots.

Accessing the diversion capabilities of airports in the NM area to support planning of alternate destinations and
tactical diversion decisions.

Accessing estimated fuel burn and emissions to assess the environmental impact of airport operations.

A graphical user interface for Tower users that brings together, in a single application, the data and operational tools
needed for collaborative decision-making between Tower, NM and the other ATFCM stakeholders.

_Within each section, Operational Needs are presented first, followed by the Operational Requirements that implement
them._
