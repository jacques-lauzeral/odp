= Flow
:notitle:

== Introduction

Flow Management is a core, cross-cutting function of the European Network: it balances traffic demand against available
capacity, applies ATFCM measures where pressure is concentrated, and coordinates the resulting actions across NMOC,
FMPs, ANSPs, Airspace Users and Airports. It is linked to trajectory management, airspace availability and overall
Network performance. This chapter expresses the Operational Needs and Operational Requirements supporting the evolution
of Flow Management within NM systems.

The chapter content is anchored in the *Flow CONOPS* and the *ASM/ATFCM Integration CONOPS*, which together provide the
main operational references for flow processes, coordination, and Network-level decision-making. Strategic alignment
derives from the *Network Strategy Plan* (in particular Strategic Objective 4.3), the *European ATM Master Plan*
(Strategic Deployment Objective 5), and the *Commission Implementing Regulation (EU) 2021/116* (CP1, in particular ATM
Functionalities 4 and 5).

Flow Management is approached across all phases of operations — demand-capacity balancing, ATFCM measures, ASM
integration, and collaborative Network management. The chapter is organised around *seven functional areas of the DCB
process*, following the natural progression from understanding capacity and demand, through assessing and implementing
solutions, to post-operations analysis. A few of the ONs that follow capture urgent operational needs carried over from
previous NM releases that were not implemented or were descoped, ensuring continuity of the baseline.

Dissemination of flow information to operational users is not addressed as a separate theme in this edition. It is
delivered through the standard NM channels — the NMUI and the NM B2B interface — in accordance with the transversal NM,
NMUI and NM B2B chapters. Specific interfaces and message flows for individual flow capabilities will be detailed
through the proper working arrangements with the operational stakeholders concerned.

The chapter content builds on two reference documents:

* link:https://www.eurocontrol.int/publication/flow-conops[*Flow CONOPS*] — describes flow management processes, coordination mechanisms and the operational concept underpinning the evolution of Flow Management.

* link:https://www.eurocontrol.int/publication/asmatfcm-integration[*ASM/ATFCM Integration CONOPS*] — describes the integration of Airspace Management with Air Traffic Flow and Capacity Management as a single coordinated process.
