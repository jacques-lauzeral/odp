= Transversal NMUI
:notitle:

The NMUI is the human-machine interface through which NM users — operators across ANSPs, Airspace Users, Airports, the
military, national authorities and NM itself — interact with the NM systems. This chapter expresses the Operational
Needs and Operational Requirements that apply transversally across the NMUI: the principles and behaviours that hold
true regardless of which operational domain the user is working in.

The NMUI is conceived as a single product. A user who works across Flight Planning, Flow Management, Airspace
Management, CCAMS or any other domain encounters the same interaction patterns, the same navigation model, the same
visual language, and the same expectations of performance and reliability. This chapter defines that shared framework.
Domain-specific chapters refine and extend it where operational specificity demands; they do not duplicate or contradict
it.
Many of the ONs gathered here are the NMUI-side refinement of transversal concerns expressed at the NM level
(chapter 2): Performance and responsiveness, Service availability, Personal data and account management, Operational
language, Time standards, Audit and legal recording, and Standard model alignment all have NM counterparts that the NMUI
inherits and adapts to the human-interface context. Cross-references make this lineage explicit.

The chapter is organised around seven themes: Foundation, Interaction principles, Working with data, Performance and
reliability, Identity and account, Help and collaboration, and Standards and traceability

_Within each section, Operational Needs are presented first, followed by the Operational Requirements that implement
them._
